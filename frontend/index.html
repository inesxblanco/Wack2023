Bath Wackathon 2022

24 hour Virtual Hackathon for Women and Gender Minorities

12th - 13th March

Signup closing 9th March

Welcome

Join us for Wackathon 2022, a 24hr virtual hackathon for Women and Gender Minorities hosted at The University of Bath. In teams of up to 4, compete to design the best technical system to solve your chosen problem against others from universities country wide. Enter to get goodies and be in with the chance of exclusive prizes through prize tracks and a much coveted spot on the podium. Register individually, and you’ll get a code to share with your team. No rush to get a team though, register today as just you to secure your spot!

With support from our industry partners there will be an opportunity for all participants to network and broaden professional contacts. Our company connections have further opportunities to fund tracks, provide goodies, judge categories and give prizes. All while meeting talented Women and Gender Minorities for their competitive career pathways.

Being a Woman or Gender Minority in technology is not always easy. With so few around, we are excited for an event designed for everyone to meet the other brilliant and powerful members of our community. Catered exactly for you, this safe, encouraging and invested space will aim to be a launchpad of learning and platform for showcasing of skills whether you’re an old hand at programming or a newbie to tech.

    FAQs

    In short, it is the ultimate technological group work task. You have 24hrs and a team to plan, design, develop and pitch a system from scratch. Whether you sacrifice functionality for ambition or strive for a fully working system the options are boundless. Pick a prize track and creatively solve the problem and you'll be real competition. As cliche as it sounds, the most important thing is have fun and meet new people.

    A prize track is a problem that needs to be solved or a question that needs to be answered.

    Tracks can be vague or specific and your project can be eligible for more than one.

    Some tracks may be funded by our industry partners, so solve those to impress the experts.

    If you or your company have a question you want to see solved or want to see students achieve in your domain please contact Adam at ald37@bath.ac.uk and they will be happy to discuss more.

    It is a pleasure to welcome Women and Gender Minorities from universities around the UK.

    If you are in Bath, our sibling student group, Bath Computer Science Society, are running Bath Hack on the 2nd-3rd April in person for all genders.

    While event sponsors have not yet been announced, we are excited to be working with some wonderful companies and institutions.

    If you or your company are also interested in supporting this event, please email Adam at ald37@bath.ac.uk and they will be happy to discuss options.

    This event will be run through a Discord server, your group will have their own channel. Talks and sessions from sponsors and experts will be run through other voice and video channels.

    For anyone lucky enough to be in Bath and the local area there will be a space on campus for UoB students to meet their group on campus.

    For students at other universities, please encourage your student groups to contact us to see if we can support an in person session at your university.

    You don't have to use the full time and we encourage you to take a break and/or sleep. But yes, 24hrs of potential project time.

    Good luck, use it wisely!
